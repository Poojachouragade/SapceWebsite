Timeline of Chandrayaans



Timeline of Chandrayaans

  • 2008
    Chandrayaan 1
    Chandrayaan-1, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 2008, was instrumental in the discovery of water molecules on the Moon's surface, challenging earlier assumptions about its dryness. The mission also detected key minerals and contributed valuable data for lunar exploration, marking a significant milestone in India's space exploration endeavors.
  • 2019
    Chandrayaan 2
    Chandrayaan-2, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 2019, aimed to further explore the Moon, with a focus on the lunar south pole region. The mission included an orbiter, lander named Vikram, and rover named Pragyan, with the orbiter continuing to provide valuable data on the Moon's surface and atmosphere after the lander encountered a hard landing..
  • 2023
    Chandrayaan 3
    Chandrayaan-3, India's latest lunar mission, successfully landed in August 2023, making it the first nation to attempt a soft landing near the moon's south pole. Though the lander Vikram had a hard landing and communications were lost, the rover Pragyan is operational and exploring the lunar surface, studying geology and searching for water ice.
  • 2026
    LUPEX
    Lupex 2026, a collaboration between India and Japan, is a planned mission to the Moon's South Pole, targeted for no earlier than 2026. It aims to map and analyze water resources in this region, using a Japanese rover and an Indian lander equipped with cutting-edge technology for surviving the harsh lunar night. This mission could unlock future possibilities for sustained lunar exploration and resource utilization.
  • 2028
    Chandrayaan 4
    Chandrayaan-4, with a planned launch in 2028, will mark India's ambitious step towards lunar sample return. Not only will it land near the south polar region, but it will also boast a rover capable of collecting lunar samples and returning them to Earth for detailed analysis. This mission will offer crucial insights into the Moon's composition and pave the way for future scientific discoveries.
